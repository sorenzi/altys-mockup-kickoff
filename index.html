Pick Mia's first quest.

Four directions for the topic-picker that runs after the avatar step. The placeholder today is an 8-emoji 2×2 grid — functional, but it doesn't sell the magic of an avatar-starring lesson being generated in the background. Each variation here trades on a different lever.

What this screen has to do
Capture a topic so the lesson can pre-render in the background. The kid's avatar already exists, so the parent should feel like they're choosing the star vehicle for a personalized lesson — not just tagging a category. Bonus points for: showing the kid's avatar/name, hinting at what the lesson will look like, and making "Not right now" feel like the loss it is. Today's design does none of those.

Variation Core idea What it sells Where it might break
AQuest Card Deck Topics as collectible tarot-style cards. The kid's name on each card. Tap to claim. Ceremony, personalization, memorability. Picking feels like an event. Fan layout hides 3 of 8 topics — discovery requires a swipe gesture parents may miss.
BCosmic Atlas Constellations on a starry sky map. Star signs match each topic's emoji shape. Light up on select. The Altos universe. Wonder. "We're going on a journey," not "answer a survey." Tap targets are constellation-shaped, not rectangular. Needs custom SVG per topic to land.
CLesson Trailer Live preview of an actual lesson at top. Topic chips below swap the storyline. Confidence. Parents see the deliverable before committing — biggest impact on completion rate. Heaviest screen — needs slide thumbnails, avatar inset rendering, and live swap polish.
DSticker Workbook Topics as 3D collectible stickers on a scrapbook page. First sticker seeds a long-term collection loop. Tactility, kid-first warmth, and a mechanic that earns its real estate (1/12 collected). Reads young — risks alienating parents of 11+. The "1/12" promise has to actually exist later.
