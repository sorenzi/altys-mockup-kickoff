Hero Stage · with auto-advance and "Meet Kai" cue

Same hero stage as before — single character, full-bleed colored backdrop, name-as-typography behind them. Two refinements address the "they might miss Kai" risk:

  • Auto-advance progress bars at the top (Instagram-stories pattern). Active bar fills over ~7s, then auto-advances to Kai. Stops on first user interaction (tap or swipe).
  • Big labeled "Meet Kai →" chip on the right edge of the stage — replaces the tiny arrow. When you're on Kai, it flips to "← Meet Luma" on the left edge. Carries a tiny color swatch so the chip itself previews the next character.

Interaction model

  • First mount: bar 1 starts filling immediately. After ~7s with no interaction, animate transition to Kai (cross-fade backdrop, slide character, name-bg swaps).
  • After Kai auto-advance: bar 2 starts filling. After another ~7s, loops back to Luma. Continues until first user interaction.
  • First user interaction (tapping chip, swiping the stage, tapping a story bar, or tapping the primary CTA): cancel auto-advance, never resume in this session. Bars become static indicators.
  • Story bars are tappable — tap a bar to jump to that character (matches the Instagram pattern kids already know).
  • Swipe horizontally on the stage → switch character (with the same cross-fade as auto-advance).
  • Primary CTA updates with active character: "Pick Luma →" / "Pick Kai →".

Kai's portrait is a stylized placeholder — final version uses the live Unity render on a color-tinted stage (same trick as the customize-character screen).
