Onboarding · Brainstorm · 2026-05-04

Character Picker · 3 directions

Goal: the kid picks a character because they vibe, not because the name sounds cool. Each variation surfaces personality through portrait, signature color, and short, specific lore — no emoji placeholders. iPad landscape uses the same two-column shape as the AB-test paywall and the Imagine sell-flow screen (left = big canvas, right = ~38% panel).

Who they are

Reference text below — fed into each variation. Specific details, not generic adjectives.

Kai

Into

Not his thing

  • group projects with people who don't read
A
Hero Stage · swipe-between-two

One character at a time, full-bleed. Tap the arrow / swipe to switch. Color-tinted backdrop morphs between characters. Use the live Unity render here later (same trick as the customize-character screen — character on a colored stage). The character's name lives behind them as a giant typographic mark, like an album cover.

C
Personality Cards · quote-led, compact

Less art-heavy than A or B. Each card is a single quote + 3 personality tags. Faster to scan, more decisive ("which voice sounds like the friend I want?"). Portrait is a side-cropped photo, not a centerpiece. Best for kids who don't want to swipe through a carousel.
